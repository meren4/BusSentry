1. PROJECT SUMMARY

1.1. Project Summary and Project Scope

In Turkey, 22% of fatal accidents involve pedestrians. This seriously affects traffic safety.
Every year, accidents occurring at pedestrian crossings, streets, and avenues increase due
to reasons such as negligence, carelessness, and inadequate infrastructure by both
pedestrians and drivers. The most common places where pedestrian accidents occur are
high-traffic areas and places such as school fronts. The main causes of these accidents
include drivers not complying with speed limits, not stopping at pedestrian crossings,
inattention, and alcohol use (Sungur, 2014). Various measures need to be taken to solve
this problem. Speed bumps can be said to be effective. However, sometimes when there
are no pedestrians, this situation is both annoying and can damage the car. To solve this
situation, we created the project called Smart Pedestrian Crossings as the critical target
group. The project is a system that replaces speed bumps and sends radio frequencies near
pedestrian crossings to slow down cars. We designed a system in which pedestrian
crossings and vehicles communicate with each other. If there is a pedestrian at the
crossing, the system sends radio frequencies to the vehicle. When the car receives the
frequencies, if it is above a certain speed, it slows down. This enables pedestrians to cross
the road comfortably.

1.2. Project Subject and Purpose

Existing measures, especially speed bumps, cannot always provide an efficient solution;
because they slow vehicles down even when there are no pedestrians and sometimes cause
mechanical damage. We developed the Smart Pedestrian Crossings project precisely to
solve this problem. Thanks to sensors placed next to the pedestrian crossing and radio
frequency technology, this system detects whether there is a pedestrian at the crossing and
communicates with vehicles. If there is a pedestrian on the road, the system sends a signal
to approaching vehicles and enables them to reduce their speed. Thus, drivers do not slow
down unnecessarily, and pedestrians can cross safely and quickly. With this smart system,
both traffic flow is preserved and pedestrian safety is raised to the highest level; in other
words, it offers a truly effective solution to prevent accidents.

3. PROBLEM / NEED SOLVED BY THE SOLUTION

3.1. Problem Definition and Literature Review

The broad outlines of the increase in accidents occurring at pedestrian crossings, streets,
and avenues are drivers not complying with speed limits, not stopping at pedestrian
crossings, carelessness, and alcohol use (Akdur, 2014). In addition, inadequate
infrastructure and the ineffectiveness of speed bumps also cause an increase in accidents.
Pedestrian crossings without illuminated signalization systems unfortunately pose a danger
to pedestrians in our country. In such pedestrian crossings, speed bumps are used to slow
down vehicles. However, the speed bumps used generally do not slow drivers down. For
this reason, the slowing system must be regulated without being left to human initiative.

3.2. Solution Idea

As a solution to this problem, we designed the Smart Pedestrian Crossings project. Our
system enables vehicles to be automatically slowed down thanks to radio transmitters
placed between pedestrian crossings. If there is a pedestrian at the crossing, radio
frequencies are sent to vehicles through signals. When vehicles detect this signal, if their
speed is above a certain limit, the system activates and reduces the vehicle’s speed. In
this way, it keeps vehicle speeds under control while pedestrians are crossing and ensures
that pedestrians can safely cross the road.

4. METHOD AND TARGET AUDIENCE

4.1. Method to Be Followed

roject Description
Since it will not be possible to present our design at full scale, we will first prepare a 3D model. For this, we will use the Tinkercad application and take outputs from our 3D printers. After the design is ready, we will place our electronic circuits inside it. The system prepared for the presentation has two stages. In both systems, the Arduino Uno will be used. PIR motion sensors will be installed to determine whether the pedestrian crossing is available.
If there is a pedestrian, signals will be sent to vehicles via RFID radio frequency transmitters. When the RFID receiver modules installed in the vehicles detect this signal, and if the speed is above a certain level, the motor driver L298N will reduce the vehicle’s speed with data received from the Arduino Uno.
The central software infrastructure to manage the system will be prepared in Arduino IDE. Tests will be conducted in areas such as school fronts and high-traffic pedestrian crossings, and the accuracy of the sensors, the response time of the vehicles, and user feedback will be evaluated. After the tests are successfully completed, the system will be made widely applicable through cooperation with municipalities and traffic management units, thus presenting a smart, sustainable, and traffic-friendly solution that enables pedestrians to cross the road safely.
Technical Summary for 3D Modeling
• Microcontroller: Arduino Uno (Central Logic)
• Sensing: PIR Motion Sensor (Pedestrian Detection)
• Communication: RFID Transmitter/Receiver (Vehicle-to-Infrastructure)
• Actuation: L298N Motor Driver (Speed Control)
• Software: C++ via Arduino IDE

4.2. Target Audience

We aim to reach pedestrians using uncontrolled pedestrian crossings without signalization
systems and the drivers of vehicles passing through them.

5. ORIGINALITY, LOCALITY, APPLICABILITY AND SUSTAINABILITY

5.1. Originality and Locality

Unlike traditional solutions, this system slows down vehicles through radio frequencies at
pedestrian crossings. Our system solves the flaws of passive methods such as speed bumps
or illuminated warning systems through active communication. The design will be made
entirely by us and will use domestic technologies. By using domestic and national Deneyap
Card and sensors in our system, we aim for 100% domestic production.

5.2. Applicability and Sustainability

The Smart Pedestrian Crossings project is designed in such a way that it can be easily
integrated into the existing traffic infrastructure, so its applicability is quite high. In addition,
it can be easily activated in increasingly common electric vehicles with a software update.
For other drivers, it can be made mandatory so that all active vehicles have the system.
To keep the system active in the long term, regular maintenance and updates can be
provided through cooperation with municipalities and traffic branch units. In addition, the
sensors can be designed to work with solar energy, increasing energy efficiency and
reducing the operating costs of the system.
