1. PROJECT SUMMARY

In traffic, whether we are pedestrians, passengers, or drivers, traffic poses a high level of risk for all elements. In order to reduce the risk level to low levels for all traffic elements, we must not forget that the rules are for everyone. Congested vehicle traffic usually increases people’s stress levels and leads them to make mistakes. This congestion becomes even worse, especially because impatient drivers try to move forward as quickly as possible. Traffic congestion increases both the risk of accidents and air pollution because the operating time of vehicles increases and exhaust gases are released into nature. The fuel consumed also means a waste of national wealth. As a team, our focus is to carry out the necessary technological interventions so that traffic can flow smoothly. We are guided by the idea that respect in traffic is everyone’s right. 2. PROBLEM / NEED SOLVED BY THE SOLUTION

While using public transportation on the way from home to school and work, we observed that other vehicle drivers park and stop in bus stop areas. For this reason, public transportation vehicles cannot approach the stops. This first creates a risk for passengers getting off public transport. Since the vehicle cannot approach the curb, the passenger has to step onto the roadway. The second and more important issue is that because the public transportation vehicle cannot approach the stop, it will obstruct the flow of traffic and cause long queues of vehicles, especially in crowded cities. To prevent parking at stops, we designed a fence-like barrier that rises from the ground to reserve stop areas, which are generally set back from the road, only for public transportation vehicles. This barrier will not create any obstruction on the road and will close downward quickly when public transportation vehicles approach the stop. In this way, since illegal parking will not occur in stop areas, congestion caused by public transportation vehicles will be eliminated.

3. LOCALITY AND ORIGINALITY

In our research, we did not come across any example related to this situation. In general, parking-prohibited areas at bus stops are only marked by painting as a warning. However, we have not encountered such a modular and practical solution.

5. METHOD TO BE FOLLOWED

Since it will not be possible to present our product at full scale, we will first prepare a 3D model. For this, we will use the Tinkercad application and produce parts with our 3D printers. After the model is ready, we will place our electronic circuits inside it. We will add an RFID module to our Arduino Uno board to open the barrier. We will prepare our Arduino board software using the C programming language.
